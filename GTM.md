# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Target User

**Engineering Manager or Staff Engineer at Series A/B SaaS** ($5M-$30M funded, <50 engineers)

- Has procurement budget authority
- Responsible for AI tool subscriptions  
- Feels cost pressure from leadership
- Can justify tool consolidation decisions
- Has team context (multiple devs, multiple tools)

---

## Discovery Channels

### Primary (Reddit + Indie Hackers)
- **r/startups** (900k members, daily cost discussions)
- **r/software_engineering** (700k members, eng mgmt topics)
- **Indie Hackers** (monthly cost optimization threads)
- **r/SideProject** (indie founders community)

### Secondary (Twitter/X)
- **#buildinpublic** (founders sharing metrics)
- **#YCombinator** (post-funding audience)
- Founder circles on X

### Tertiary (Slack Groups)
- **Indie Hackers Slack** (50k members, tools/costs channel)
- Founder-specific communities
- YC Startup School alumni

---

## Launch Plan: $0 Budget, 100 Users in 30 Days

### Week 1: Seed Community (Target: 20 users)

**r/startups:** "I built a free AI spend audit tool after talking to 3 founders who didn't realize they were overspending."
- Real quotes from interviews
- Screenshot of a sample audit
- Expected: 8-12 users

**Indie Hackers:** "Free AI spend audit. No signup required, shareable results."
- Screenshot of clean results page
- Mention it took 5 days to build
- Expected: 8-12 users

**Twitter DMs:** 10 founders on X with personalized 1-liner
- Expected: 1-3 responses, 1-2 users

### Week 2-3: Amplification (Target: 50-60 cumulative)
- Ask 3-5 founder friends to share
- Post in 2-3 founder Slack groups
- Follow-up Reddit post: "Built for free, got 20 users. Here's what I learned..."

### Week 4: Credex Amplification (Target: 80-100+)
- Credex mentions in their newsletter/social (unfair distribution advantage)
- Partner shares to their audience

---

## Unfair Advantage

**Credex's existing founder network:** The tool gets 10x distribution because Credex can legitimately say "Our team built this to help founders" in their community channels. This is different from cold outreach—it's co-founder credibility.
